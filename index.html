The Closed Loop of Truth

Institutional failure rarely begins as a catastrophe. It starts quietly — an unauthorised deduction, a board resolution passed without risk assessment, a whistleblower silenced. These micro‑breaches accumulate like plaque in an artery until the institution suffers a governance cardiac arrest everyone claims was "unforeseeable."

The Wilbur Method™ treats governance failure not as a moral shortcoming but as a structural pathology to be surgically removed.

"Just as a clinician reads vital signs, runs diagnostic scans, identifies the pathogen, and applies targeted treatment — the Wilbur Method™ audits the paper trail, identifies the exact coordinates of statutory breach, traces the root cause to its structural origin, and applies the statutory remedy with surgical precision."

Three Points of Alignment

01. The Proclaimed Policy

What the entity says it does — its constitution, board resolutions, and public commitments.

02. The Paper Trail

What the data proves it actually did — time‑stamped, verifiable, and irrefutable.

03. The Statutory Obligation

What the law mandates must be done — Companies Act, PFMA, Protected Disclosures Act, and sector‑specific legislation.

Any divergence between these three points opens the loop. An open loop is the diagnostic signature of Institutional Sepsis.

The Statutory Remedy Matrix

Every breach mapped to its binding statutory remedy. The Ethic Hawk never improvises a legal remedy.

Category A: Corporate Governance

Director breach of good faith → s.76(3) Companies Act → Personal liability, removal s.162, disqualification s.69 → High Court

Irregular expenditure approval → PFMA s.38 → Personal surcharge, delinquency → Auditor‑General

Category B: Labour & Whistleblower

Unlawful deduction >25% BCEA cap → BCEA s.34(1) → Repayment order, damages → Labour Court

Occupational detriment post‑disclosure → PDA s.3 / LRA s.185 → Reinstatement, compensation, burden reversal → CCMA / Labour Court

Category C: Clinical Governance

Failure to report patient safety incident → NHA s.79 / OHSC → HPCSA referral, Health Ombud

Suppression of adverse event data → HP Act s.41 → Professional sanction, criminal referral → NPA
